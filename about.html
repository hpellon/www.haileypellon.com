Projects

Affordable Care Act: 2015 Employer Shared Responsibility Reporting

The Affordable Care Act Employer Shared Responsibility Reporting requires employers to submit information regarding their full-time employees (anyone who worked over 130 hours per month) along with proof that the employer offered of minimum essential medical coverage to the same employees. If the employer did not offer minimum coverage to qualifying employees, the organization is responsible for paying an Employer Shared Responsibility Payment. The IRS mandated that all employers follow the Employer Share Responsibility provision and report data for the 2015 calendar year in 2016.

Gathering benefit coverage data and work hours for over 10,000 employees for each month in 2015 from multiple systems proved a difficult feat that required the collaboration of other internal departments and multiple vendors. I worked proactively while managing the daily activities of the project, vendors, and partners to meet the employee and IRS reporting deadlines with the most accurate data available.

Baker Hughes BEACON: Chevron Remote Drilling Optimization Center Project

BEACON (under Baker Hughes’ Remote Operations Service division) uses Wellsite Information Transfer Standard Markup Language (WITSML) to provide real-time drilling data to clients globally for more accurate and safe drilling operations management. Working as Business Analyst on pioneering team on a multi-million dollar project to create a secure and dedicated real-time well data monitoring center for Chevron’s new Remote Drilling Optimization Center Project, I oversaw Service Level Agreement compliance, billing data, and staff scheduling as well as the construction of Chevron dedicated monitoring facility.

Houston ISD: Apollo 20 Program

The Houston ISD Apollo 20 Fellows Program aims to improve the academic performance of students among the Districts most impoverished communities in an effort to close the achievement gap. By increasing the quality and length of instruction, Apollo 20 is preparing students for a successful future in the college or career of their choice. I worked with the Apollo Program at Sharpstown High School as a full-time Math Tutor, helping below grade-level students grasp fundamental math concepts to excel in their regular education classes while encouraging regular attendance and future planning.

Sharpstown High School was featured on PBS’s Frontline episode “Dropout Nation.”
